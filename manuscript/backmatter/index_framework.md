## Mathematical Objects and Notation

**$\mathbf{a}(v_i)$ (attribute vector)**, 4 --- 9-dimensional vector scoring clinical-moral state at vertex $v_i$
**$\beta_k$ (boundary penalty)**, 4, 7, 13 --- cost of crossing clinical-moral boundary $k$; $\beta_{\text{sacred}} = \infty$
**$\text{BI}(P, S)$ (clinical Bond Index)**, 12, 16, 17 --- $\mathbb{E}[\text{CF}(\gamma_P^*) - \text{CF}(\gamma_C^*)]$; structural injustice measure
**$\mathcal{C}$ (clinical decision complex)**, 4, 6, 10 --- 9-dimensional weighted simplicial complex
**$\text{CF}(\gamma)$ (clinical friction)**, 10, 12 --- total path cost on the clinical decision complex
**$d_1$--$d_9$ (clinical-moral dimensions)**, 4 --- outcomes, rights, justice, autonomy, trust, social, dignity, institutional, epistemic
**$D_4$ (dihedral group)**, 4 --- symmetry of Hohfeldian relations on $d_2$
**$f(n) = g(n) + h(n)$ (evaluation function)**, 6, 7 --- total clinical evaluation; $g$ = EBM, $h$ = clinical wisdom
**$\gamma^*$ (clinical geodesic)**, 6, 9, 10, 12 --- minimum-cost path on the full 9-dimensional manifold
**$\text{MI}(T)$ (cumulative moral injury)**, 13 --- $\sum_{t=1}^T \Delta \text{MI}(t)$; monotonically non-decreasing
**$\text{MI}(P)$ (Moral Injury Index)**, 13, 15, 17 --- expected MI imposed by policy $P$ on clinician population
**$\Sigma$ (clinical-moral covariance matrix)**, 4, 5, 17, 18 --- $9 \times 9$ SPD matrix; critical cross-terms $\Sigma_{15}$, $\Sigma_{49}$, $\Sigma_{37}$
**$w(v_i, v_j)$ (clinical edge weight)**, 4, 6 --- Mahalanobis distance + boundary penalties

## Key Theorems and Results

**Clinical Heuristic Admissibility Theorem (Thm. 7.1)**, 7 --- boundary penalties calibrated as lower bounds imply admissible $h(n)$ and optimal $A^*$ search
**Consent as Gauge Invariance (Thm. 8.1)**, 8 --- informed consent is equivalent to gauge-invariance of the patient's clinical decision under meaning-preserving reframing
**Divergence of Utilitarian and Manifold-Optimal Triage (Thm. 10.1)**, 10 --- utilitarian and manifold-optimal triage coincide only when decisions activate $d_1$ alone and no boundaries are crossed
**Moral Injury Accumulation Theorem (Thm. 13.1)**, 13 --- MI is cumulative, irreversible above threshold, and structurally distinct from burnout
**QALY Irrecoverability Theorem (Thm. 5.1)**, 5 --- any scalar QALY function is non-injective; information loss is irrecoverable; QALY-optimal path diverges from clinical geodesic
**Scalar Irrecoverability Theorem (General)**, 1, 5, App. A --- any $\mathbb{R}^n \to \mathbb{R}$ function ($n > 1$) destroys irrecoverable information
